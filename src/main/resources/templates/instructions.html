Instructions

  1. Create a New Account:

    2. Start by signing up for a new account with your details.

  2. Go to Dashboard:

    3. After signing up, click on the "Go to Dashboard" button.

  3. Confirm Your Account Number:

    4. Make sure to check and confirm your account number on the dashboard. It's important to know your account number.

  4. Sign In:

    5. Log in to your account with your account number

  5. Choose from 3 Options:

    6. Once you're signed in, you'll see three options:

    • Deposit: To add money to your account.
    • Withdraw: To take money out of your account.
    • Check Balance: To see how much money is in your account.
  6. Delete Your Account (Optional):

    7. If you want to delete your account, look for a red button. Click it to delete your account (but be careful, this will remove all your account information).

Certain Instructions

  1. Front Page:

    2. Create a New Account:

    On the front page, you'll find a form.

    It only accepts 8-10 digit numbers as your legitimate account number.

    Enter your account number and balance to create a new account.

  2. Dashboard Page:

    3. Deposit Money:

    On the dashboard page, you can deposit money into your account.

    You can also deposit money into other accounts if needed.

    The money you deposit will be deducted from your account balance.

    Withdraw Money:

    You can only withdraw money from your own account.

    Just click the withdrawal option.

    Enter the amount you want to withdraw.

    Check Your Balance:

    You can check your own account balance on the dashboard.

    It helps you keep track of your money.

  3. Delete Your Account:

    4. If you decide to close your account, there's an option for that.

    Be sure you really want to do this because it will remove all your account information.

Technology Used

  1. Creating a Java Web Development with Spring Boot, HTML, CSS, and Static Files:

    2. Spring Boot (Java Web Development):

    • I used Spring Boot to create my web project.
    • I made different classes and interfaces for the "backend" (where the magic happens) and the "MVC" technique (which connects the "frontend" to the "backend").

    HTML (Frontend):

    • In Spring Boot, there's a built-in feature called Thymeleaf for the frontend.
    • I used Thymeleaf to create different HTML pages for my website.

    CSS (Website Styling):

    • I used CSS to make my website look good and maintain a professional appearance.
    • CSS helped me with things like colors, fonts, and layouts.

    Static Files (Front Page Image):

    • I stored a single image in the "static" folder to make my front page look attractive.
    • This image is used to make a good first impression when people visit my website.
